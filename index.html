Floods, Rainfall and Losses Across Malaysia

Floods are one of Malaysia’s most frequent natural hazards, often linked to seasonal rainfall patterns. This dashboard provides an overview of flood occurrences across Malaysian states.

The map displays relative flood frequency per state, normalized against the state with the highest flood count.

Click on a state to explore its monthly rainfall for the selected year.

Floods and Monthy Rainfall in Malaysia

Rainfall Variability

Malaysia’s rainfall patterns vary widely across regions due to differing exposure to the Northeast Monsoon.

The box plot visualizes how Monsoon-Affected regions generally experience higher rainfall medians and wider variability compared to less affected states.

The broader interquartile ranges for eastern coastal states suggest greater day-to-day rainfall fluctuations, aligning with seasonal monsoon impacts.

Flood Conservation and Emergency Losses (2022 vs 2023)

From the chart, it is clear that overall losses decreased significantly from 2022 to 2023 in most states. For example, Selangor recorded a sharp drop in total losses, suggesting improved flood control measures or fewer severe flood events in 2023.

However, not all states followed this trend. Perak experienced a notable increase in total loss, mainly driven by higher emergency losses, indicating localised flood incidents or new vulnerabilities emerging in 2023.

In terms of loss composition, most states in 2022 had higher conservation losses, meaning more resources were allocated to recovery and preventive measures. In 2023, this balance shifted towards emergency losses, suggesting that immediate response costs remained relatively high in certain regions.

Overall, the chart highlights an encouraging pattern, nationwide flood impacts are declining, but continued attention is needed in states to sustain this improvement.
